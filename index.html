Overview

Video caption goes here

The Capstone Project for the Masters of Human-Computer Interaction program at Carnegie Mellon University is a two semester long dive into a client-identified, existing design problem. Interdisciplinary teams of 5 students research, envision, and design a solution for their client. Our client, Odigo, is a Tokyo-based technology startup that provides a digital platform for creating and sharing customized trip itineraries for foreigners who visit Japan. Odigo tasked us with the goal of increasing the number of users to their platform. We researched the foreign tourist's journey to Japan and ...

With its ancient history, magnetic culture, unique cuisine, and natural beauty, Japan attracts tourists from all over the world. However, cultural, linguistic, and logistical barriers inhibit foreign tourists’ ability to fully enjoy Japan.

Design Challenge

Odigo’s original task for this project centered on designing an interactive experience to attract tourists to Expo City, a suburban entertainment and shopping complex near Osaka, Japan. Their goal was to increase the number of users of their website and mobile platform.

Create a product or service prototype to supplement the Odigo ecosystem that will help tourists in Japan overcome barriers or create moments of delight through their journey. The solution will enhance Odigo’s user base and bring recognition to their brand.

  • Interviewing Tourists

    To understand the journey of the foreign tourist to Japan, we interviewed a total of 29 individuals across the U.S. who had been to Japan before.

  • Ethnographic Research in Japan

    We travelled to Japan from Pittsburgh to understand the Western tourist's journey, build empathy, conduct user interviews, and perform participant observation.

  • Competitive Analysis

    We studied Odigo’s competitors, from TripAdvisor to Lonely Planet, to better understand Odigo’s advantage in the space of Japanese Tourism.

  • Card Sorting

    We created and used cards to test assumptions about travel with 5 participants. We learned assumptions people have about Japan, what images of Japan excited them, and how tourists expect to handle frustrating situations.

  • Social Media Analysis

    We collected images from Instagram to get a sense of popular attractions and tourist interests prior to leaving for Japan.

  • Affinity Diagramming

    We compiled and sorted 1900+ notes from our interviews and observations into one colossal affinity diagram.
