Research

  • Interviewing Tourists

    To understand the journey of the foreign tourist to Japan, we interviewed a total of 29 individuals across the U.S. who had been to Japan before.

  • Ethnographic Research in Japan

    We travelled to Japan from Pittsburgh to understand the Western tourist's journey, build empathy, conduct user interviews, and perform participant observation.

  • Competitive Analysis

    We studied Odigo’s competitors, from TripAdvisor to Lonely Planet, to better understand Odigo’s advantage in the space of Japanese Tourism.

  • Card Sorting

    We created and used cards to test assumptions about travel with 5 participants. We learned assumptions people have about Japan, what images of Japan excited them, and how tourists expect to handle frustrating situations.

  • Social Media Analysis

    We collected images from Instagram to get a sense of popular attractions and tourist interests prior to leaving for Japan.

Opportunity Areas

Opportunity 1

Develop experiences that support tourists’ desire to participate in Japanese cultural activities

Opportunity 2

Give aid in situations where tourists are confused by social etiquette in Japan

Opportunity 3

Give tourists a resource to navigate the language barrier beyond just pure translation

Opportunity 4

Create connections that help tourists travel to and within Japan

Ideation

We collected a range of pain points tourists face throughout their journey to Japan and brainstormed ways of addressing them as quickly. Ultimately, we created a total of 21 visions, each with its own unique and occasionally crazy solution to a pain point.

Speed dating

From the 21 ideas, we distilled 10 ideas to speed date. Speed dating is a method of gathering concept validation from users before prototyping. We created short video vignettes to encapsulate each idea. Using these videos, we conducted in-person and remote sessions with users and Odigo employees in order to gather feedback on the ideas. In total, we collected feedback from 19 users and 5 Odigo employees.
